Baby Milestones

Milestones: Birth to 3 Months

During the first three months, your baby begins adjusting to the world outside the womb. These months are wholesome and tiresome at the same time. They are characterized by rapid physical and sensory development.

Physical Development:

  • Shows reflexive movements such as grasping or the startle reflex.
  • Starts lifting their head during tummy time.
  • Develops more controlled arm and leg movements.

Sensory and Cognitive Development:

  • Focuses on objects or faces within 8–12 inches.
  • Tracks objects with their eyes briefly.
  • Begins to respond to sounds by turning their head.

Social and Emotional Development:

  • Smiles in response to familiar faces or voices.
  • Starts making cooing or gurgling sounds.

Caregiver tips:

Use soft toys, tummy time, and gentle interactions to help build physical and emotional bonds.

Milestones: 4-6 Months

By this stage, babies become more active, curious, and responsive. They also develop strength and better motor skills. Some babies starts sleeping better at night than before, since they start differentiating night and day.

Physical Development:

  • Rolls over from tummy to back.
  • Reaches for and grabs objects deliberately.
  • Begins sitting with little support.

Sensory and Cognitive Development:

  • Explores surroundings by mouthing objects.
  • Recognizes familiar faces from a distance.
  • Starts to laugh and giggle.

Social and Emotional Development:

  • Expresses enjoyment during playtime.
  • Shows interest in other people or objects in the environment.

Caregiver tips:

Encourage exploration by offering colorful toys, safe mirrors, and engaging sounds to stimulate learning.

Milestones: 7-9 Months

Between 7-9 months, your baby becomes more mobile, often crawling or scooting, and starts pulling up to stand. They sit steadily, pass toys between hands, and explore objects with curiosity. Socially, they form deeper bonds, may show separation anxiety, and respond to their name and simple instructions. This is a time of exciting growth, filled with discovery and new skills!

Physical Development:

  • Crawls or scoots to move around.
  • Sits steadily without support.
  • Starts to pull up to a standing position

Sensory and Cognitive Development:

  • Develops the ability to transfer objects between hands.
  • Shows curiosity by exploring objects and their uses.
  • Understands simple actions like waving goodbye.

Social and Emotional Development:

  • Experiences separation anxiety when away from caregivers.
  • Recognizes and responds to their name.

Caregiver tips:

Play games like peek-a-boo and provide safe spaces to explore crawling and pulling up to stand.

Milestones: 10-12 Months

Between 10-12 months, your baby may take their first steps, refine motor skills like stacking objects, and begin imitating sounds or gestures. They start understanding simple instructions and use gestures or babbling to communicate, marking an exciting transition toward toddlerhood.

Physical Development:

  • May take first independent steps. May because children develop differently. Don't panic if your child hasn't started walking at this time, give him/her time.
  • Masters fine motor skills like grasping and releasing objects.
  • Enjoys climbing on low surfaces.

Sensory and Cognitive Development:

  • Begins imitating simple actions or sounds.
  • Understands basic instructions like “come here.”
  • Stacks objects or places them inside containers.

Social and Emotional Development:

  • Develops stronger bonds with family members.
  • Expresses a wider range of emotions such as excitement or frustration.

Caregiver tips:

Support their walking with encouragement, practice simple words for communication, and enjoy interactive activities that nurture their development.
