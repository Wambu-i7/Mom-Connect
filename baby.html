Baby Milestones

Milestones: Birth to 3 Months

During the first three months, your baby begins adjusting to the world outside the womb. These months are wholesome and tiresome at the same time. They are characterized by rapid physical and sensory development.

Physical Development:

  • Shows reflexive movements such as grasping or the startle reflex.
  • Starts lifting their head during tummy time.
  • Develops more controlled arm and leg movements.

Sensory and Cognitive Development:

  • Focuses on objects or faces within 8–12 inches.
  • Tracks objects with their eyes briefly.
  • Begins to respond to sounds by turning their head.

Social and Emotional Development:

  • Smiles in response to familiar faces or voices.
  • Starts making cooing or gurgling sounds.

Use soft toys, tummy time, and gentle interactions to help build physical and emotional bonds.

Milestones: 4-6 Months

By this stage, babies become more active, curious, and responsive. They also develop strength and better motor skills. Some babies starts sleeping better at night than before, since they start differentiating night and day.

Physical Development:

  • Rolls over from tummy to back.
  • Reaches for and grabs objects deliberately.
  • Begins sitting with little support.

Sensory and Cognitive Development:

  • Explores surroundings by mouthing objects.
  • Recognizes familiar faces from a distance.
  • Starts to laugh and giggle.

Social and Emotional Development:

  • Expresses enjoyment during playtime.
  • Shows interest in other people or objects in the environment.

Encourage exploration by offering colorful toys, safe mirrors, and engaging sounds to stimulate learning.

Milestones: 7-9 Months

The third trimester lasts from week 28 until birth. It's a time of final preparations and rapid growth for the baby.

Between pregnancy weeks 28 and 40, the baby undergoes rapid growth and prepares for birth. By week 28, the baby weighs about 1 kg (2 lb 2 oz), measures around 25 cm (10 inches) from crown to rump, and 37 cm from crown to toe, with its body now in proportion to its head. At week 32, the baby spends most of its time asleep, moves strongly and in a coordinated manner, and has likely assumed the head-down position in preparation for birth. By week 36, the baby is about 46 cm long and may have nestled its head into the mother’s pelvis, significantly increasing its chances of survival if born, as lung development rapidly progresses in the following weeks. By week 40, the baby measures around 51 cm and is fully ready to be born, though the exact cause of labor remains unknown and is believed to result from a combination of physical, hormonal, and emotional factors between the mother and baby.

Milestones: 10-12 Months

The second trimester lasts from week 13 to week 27. This is when many women feel their best and the baby continues to grow and develop.

This time the morning sickness eases and you may feel energetic.

  • Engage in light exercise.
  • Take childbirth classes.
  • Attend prenatal appointments.
  • Eat healthy foods now that the morning sickness has lessen.

Between pregnancy weeks 13 and 24, significant developmental milestones occur for the fetus. At week 13, the fetus, now over 7 cm long, swims vigorously. By week 14, its eyelids are fused over fully developed eyes, vocal cords form, and it may start sucking its thumb while fingers and toes grow nails. At week 16, the fetus measures around 14 cm, with the appearance of eyelashes, eyebrows, and taste buds on the tongue; this is also when second-trimester maternal serum screening may be offered if it wasn’t done earlier. Between weeks 18 and 20, a detailed ultrasound scan is conducted to check fetal development, placenta position, and multiple pregnancies; fetal hiccups can often be observed. By week 20, the fetus is about 21 cm, can hear muffled outside sounds, and its genitals are visible via ultrasound, with unique fingerprints also forming. At week 24, the fetus grows to 33 cm, begins to open and close its eyes as fused eyelids separate, and its skin becomes covered in fine hair (lanugo) and a protective waxy layer (vernix). The baby also starts practicing breathing movements with its lungs, preparing for life outside the womb.
